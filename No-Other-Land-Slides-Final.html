03 — Subject
Masafer Yatta
South Hebron Hills
  • Five years of vérité footage (2019–2023)
  • Systematic home demolitions by Israeli military
  • Community displacement from "Firing Zone 918"
  • Settler violence against residents
Key Legal Context

In 2022, the Israeli Supreme Court ruled to allow expulsion of approximately 1,000 residents from land they had farmed for generations.

06 — Distribution
The Distribution Problem
The Gap
Despite the Oscar win and distribution in 24 countries, the film had no U.S. distributor for over a year.
Documented Suppression
  • UCLA: Hillel withdrew sponsorship; venue canceled
  • Miami Beach: Mayor attempted to terminate theater lease
  • India: Pulled from Mumbai Film Festival
  • Germany: Acceptance speeches called "antisemitic"
The difficulty of screening this film is part of the text.

10 — Viewing Mode

Experiential
Allow yourself to be moved, disturbed, affected.
Analytical
Attend to construction, choice, and absence.
These modes are not in conflict — move between them freely.
11 — Analytical Anchors
Questions of Form
  • Time: How does five years of accumulation shape the argument?
  • Collapsing roles: Directors are also subjects — what does this enable?
  • Vérité: What do handheld immediacy and the absence of narration produce that exposition cannot?
12 — Analytical Anchors
Questions of Perspective
  • Whose voices are centered? Whose are absent?
  • How does the film handle the tension between documenting harm and the limits of documentation?
  • What does the U.S. distribution gap tell us about which perspectives circulate freely?
13 — A Note on Response
Watching Well
Legitimate to have
  • Strong emotional reactions
  • Prior knowledge or beliefs
  • Disagreement with the film's framing
Required
  • Watch with full attention
  • Disagree based on what is in the film, not what is imagined
  • Treat depicted events as real — they are documented by UN, B'Tselem, Human Rights Watch
